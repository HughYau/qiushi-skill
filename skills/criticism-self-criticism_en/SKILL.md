# Criticism and Self-Criticism

> "Dust will accumulate if a room is not cleaned regularly, our faces will get dirty if they are not washed regularly. Our comrades' minds and our Party's work may also collect dust, and also need sweeping and washing."
> — Mao Zedong, "On Coalition Government"《论联合政府》(1945)

## Core Principle

**Regular, honest self-examination and mutual criticism are the only mechanism for preventing the accumulation of errors. The purpose of criticism is not punishment, but "curing the sickness to save the patient."**

> For detailed source references, see original-texts.md

## When NOT to Use

**This skill is not needed when:**
- The task has just begun and there are no work products to examine yet
- The user is providing real-time guidance and feedback is being incorporated immediately — no need for after-the-fact critique
- A simple single-step task (e.g., "look up this function's signature for me") — there is no process to examine
- You are in the execution phase of an urgent fix — complete the fix first, then review afterward

## When to Use

You should invoke this skill when:

- After completing a piece of work, you need to examine its quality
- You have received criticism or feedback from others
- You suspect your work may have issues but are not certain
- The project has reached a milestone and needs a retrospective
- The same type of error keeps recurring and you need to find the root cause
- You need to establish a regular review mechanism for a team/project

## Method Workflow

### Step 1: Self-Criticize First

> "We must expose past errors without regard for personal feelings" — "Rectify the Party's Style of Work"《整顿党的作风》

Before asking anyone else to review, honestly examine your own work:
- Did I achieve my work objectives?
- Where in the process did I fall short?
- Were there places where I cut corners, was careless, or took shortcuts?
- Were there issues I knew about but chose to ignore?
- If I could start over, where would I make different choices?

**Key discipline: Do not conceal ailments to avoid treatment.** Problems you already know about must be proactively exposed, not hidden away.

### Step 2: Be Specific, Not Vague

> Criticism must involve "analyzing and critiquing with a scientific attitude" — "Rectify the Party's Style of Work"《整顿党的作风》

Both self-criticism and mutual criticism must be specific:
- **Wrong example:** "I feel it wasn't good enough" (vague, impossible to improve upon)
- **Right example:** "In step 3, I skipped the validation step, which led to problem X downstream" (specific, actionable)

You must:
- Point to specific behaviors or decisions, not vague character assessments
- Analyze the causes of errors, not merely describe symptoms
- Explain the impact of the errors
- Propose specific methods for improvement

### Step 3: Base Criticism on Facts, Not Speculation

Criticism must be grounded in investigation (`investigation-first`):
- Cite specific facts and evidence
- Do not rely on hearsay or speculation
- Mark areas of uncertainty as "unconfirmed"
- Allow the criticized party to provide supplementary information

### Step 4: Cure the Sickness to Save the Patient, Not Punish

> "The purpose is entirely to save the person, not to cure them to death." — "Rectify the Party's Style of Work"《整顿党的作风》

The purpose of criticism is improvement, not blame:
- For every problem identified, propose an improvement suggestion
- While acknowledging problems, also affirm what was done well
- Focus on "how to do better next time," not "whose fault this was"
- Attitude toward errors: mistakes are opportunities for learning, not sources of shame

### Step 5: Welcome Criticism from Others

> "Because we serve the people, we are not afraid of having our shortcomings pointed out by others. It doesn't matter who points them out. As long as you are right, we will correct ourselves." — "Serve the People"《为人民服务》

When receiving criticism:
- Listen fully first; do not rush to defend yourself
- Assess whether the criticism is based on facts
- If the criticism is correct — accept it immediately and make corrections
- If the criticism is inaccurate — explain with facts and reasoning, not emotional responses
- Thank the person who offered the criticism (even if it was uncomfortable)

### Step 6: Establish a Regular Mechanism

> "Dust will accumulate if a room is not cleaned regularly" — "On Coalition Government"《论联合政府》

Criticism and self-criticism are not emergency measures but a routine mechanism:
- Conduct a self-review after completing each phase of work
- Conduct retrospectives regularly (not only when problems arise)
- Make issues found in the previous critique a focus for the next review
- Accumulate error records to identify patterns and root causes

## Self-Criticism Checklist

After completing work, self-check with the following questions:

- [ ] Did I achieve the original objectives?
- [ ] Did I skip any steps I should have taken during the process?
- [ ] Was my solution validated through practice (`practice-cognition`)?
- [ ] Did I thoroughly investigate the situation (`investigation-first`)?
- [ ] Did I collect opinions from relevant parties (`mass-line`)?
- [ ] Did I identify the principal contradiction (`contradiction-analysis`)?
- [ ] Are there issues I am aware of but have been avoiding?
- [ ] If I could start over, what would I do differently?

## Two Erroneous Tendencies

### Unprincipled Criticism (Attacking the Person, Not the Problem)

Manifestations:
- Criticism targets someone's character rather than specific behaviors
- Criticism serves no constructive purpose, merely venting frustration
- Using "criticism" as a pretext to suppress differing opinions

This violates the principle of "curing the sickness to save the patient."

### Refusing Criticism (Concealing Ailments to Avoid Treatment)

> "As long as they do not conceal their ailments to avoid treatment or persist in their errors until they are beyond cure" — "Rectify the Party's Style of Work"《整顿党的作风》

Manifestations:
- Rushing to defend yourself upon hearing criticism
- Treating valid criticism as an "attack"
- Selectively accepting criticism (only accepting what is pleasant to hear)
- Accepting on the surface but not actually making changes

This is the most dangerous tendency — errors will only accumulate.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Mao's Critique | Correct Approach |
|------|------------|---------|
| Assuming perfection, not self-checking | "Dust will accumulate if a room is not cleaned" | Self-check is mandatory after completing work |
| Vague, non-specific criticism | Must "analyze and critique with a scientific attitude" | Point to specific behaviors and impacts |
| Criticism aimed at assigning blame | "The purpose is entirely to save the person" | The purpose of criticism is improvement |
| Refusing to listen to criticism | "It doesn't matter who points them out" | Welcome all valid criticism |
| Reflecting only when problems arise | "Should be cleaned regularly" | Establish a regular self-review mechanism |
| Accepting on the surface but not correcting | "Willing to be treated, willing to correct" | Acceptance must be followed by actual correction |

## Operating Procedure

When this skill is triggered, **a structured review report must be produced** — vague assessments alone are not acceptable:

```
## Work Review Report

### Original Objectives
[Restate the original goals and acceptance criteria]

### Completion Status
- [x] Completed: ...
- [ ] Not completed: ... (Reason: ...)

### Issues Found
| Severity | Issue Description (specific behavior, not vague) | Root Cause | Improvement Suggestion |
|---------|-------------------------------|---------|---------|
| Must fix | ... | ... | ... |
| Should fix | ... | ... | ... |
| Suggested improvement | ... | ... | ... |

### What Was Done Well
- ...

### Focus Areas for Next Time
- ...
```

**Filling rules:**
- "Issue Description" must include specific step numbers or file names; writing vague statements like "not good enough" is prohibited
- "Root Cause" must point to a methodological failure (skipped investigation? no validation? attention was scattered?)
- If the "Issues Found" table is empty, explicitly state: "No issues requiring correction were found in this work, because..."

> 📌 **Follow-up closure**: After the report is generated, output the "Focus Areas for Next Time" section directly to the user, and inform them: "To implement these improvements in your next task, paste this report as context at the beginning of the conversation." — AI cannot retain memory across sessions; this is the only reliable follow-up mechanism.

## Relationships with Other Skills

- **Contradiction Analysis**: Use contradiction analysis to identify the principal issues in the work
- **Practice-Cognition**: Self-criticism is the "summarize experience" phase in the practice-cognition cycle
- **Investigation First**: Criticism must be grounded in facts, requiring support from investigation
- **Mass Line**: Receiving criticism from the masses is an essential component of the mass line

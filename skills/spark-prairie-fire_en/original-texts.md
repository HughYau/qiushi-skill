# A Single Spark Can Start a Prairie Fire — Original Textual Basis

### "A Single Spark Can Start a Prairie Fire"《星星之火，可以燎原》(January 1930)

**Opposing pessimism:**
> "Although it is presently but a tiny force, its development will be very rapid."
> "Marxists are not fortune-tellers. They should and can only indicate the general direction of future developments and changes; they should not and cannot fix the day and hour in a mechanistic way."

**Establishing base areas rather than roving banditry:**
> Mao Zedong criticized the thinking of simple "roving guerrilla warfare" and advocated building consolidated base areas — development bases with organization, foundations, and mass support. The problem with the mentality of roving banditry was: no roots, drifting from place to place, with no way to accumulate strength.

**Analysis of conditions:**
> Whether something can develop depends on whether the objective conditions are in place. Whether a "single spark" can "start a prairie fire" depends on whether the "dry timber" is already present — that is, whether the objective conditions have matured.

### "On Establishing a Reporting System"《关于建立报告制度》(January 1948)

> A systematic reporting system and organizational discipline are the foundation of base area construction — scattered actions have no cumulative value.

### "Problems of Strategy in China's Revolutionary War"《中国革命战争的战略问题》(December 1936)

> "The endpoint of a strategic retreat... should be selected in a place advantageous to us and disadvantageous to the enemy." — The choice of base areas must be made with strategic vision.

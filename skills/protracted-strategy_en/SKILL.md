# Protracted Strategy

> "Strategically we should despise all our enemies, but tactically we should take them all seriously. That is to say, with regard to the whole we must despise the enemy, but with regard to each specific problem we must take him seriously."
> — Mao Zedong (1958)

## Core Principle

**When facing complex, long-term tasks, neither rush for quick victory (quick-victory-ism) nor give up in fear of difficulty (defeatism). Instead, divide the work into stages and advance steadily. Maintain confidence strategically; treat every step with rigor tactically.**

> For detailed source references, see original-texts.md

## When NOT to Use

**This skill is not needed when:**
- The task can be completed within the current session — no need for a phased strategy; just finish it
- The user only needs the result of a single specific step
- The problem already has a clear solution and only lacks execution — use `concentrate-forces` to finish it
- You are in the middle of executing a specific phase and do not need to reassess strategy

## When to Use

You should invoke this skill when:

- Facing a task that clearly requires a long time to complete
- Currently at a disadvantage (insufficient resources, information, or capability)
- Encountering a bottleneck mid-task, feeling anxious or tempted to give up
- Needing to plan a phased advancement strategy
- Others are pressing for quick completion, but you judge it cannot be rushed
- Needing to preserve strength while continuing to make progress

## Method Workflow

### Step 1: Objectively Assess the Situation

> "Whether the subjective guidance is correct or not... affects the outcome of war." — "On Protracted War"《论持久战》

Conduct a comprehensive, objective assessment of the task:

**Our conditions:**
- Currently available resources, capabilities, and information
- Results and progress already achieved
- Existing weaknesses and deficiencies

**Task conditions:**
- Overall scale and difficulty of the task
- Foreseeable difficulties and obstacles
- Favorable conditions and opportunities

**Key point:** Neither exaggerate difficulties (leading to pessimism) nor underestimate them (leading to recklessness). Seek truth from facts.

### Step 2: Reject Both Quick-Victory-ism and Defeatism

> Oppose both "national-subjugation theory" and "quick-victory theory" — "On Protracted War"《论持久战》

Make a clear-headed judgment:
- **Quick-victory-ism looks like:** "This is simple, we can knock it out right away" or "Let's push through and finish it all at once"
- **Defeatism looks like:** "This is too complex, it can't be done" or "This is beyond our capability"
- **The correct attitude:** "This requires sustained effort, but it can ultimately be accomplished. The key is to advance in stages."

### Step 3: Divide into Stages

> Strategic defense → Strategic stalemate → Strategic counteroffensive — "On Protracted War"《论持久战》

Divide the long-term task into three stages:

**Stage One · Strategic Defense (Exploration and Accumulation):**
- Objective: Gain a foothold, preserve strength, build understanding
- Method: Investigation first (`investigation-first`), establish foundational knowledge
- Mindset: Do not rush for results; allow "retreat" to preserve strength
- Key action: Do the small things you can, accumulate first-hand experience

**Stage Two · Strategic Stalemate (The Most Difficult Yet Most Critical):**
- Objective: Gradually accumulate advantages, await the turning point
- Method: Accumulate small victories into a major one; concentrate forces on each specific problem (`concentrate-forces`)
- Mindset: Patience — this is "the most difficult period, yet it is the pivot of the whole situation"
- Key action: Continue advancing; do not lose heart because of slow progress

**Stage Three · Strategic Counteroffensive (Breakthrough and Harvest):**
- Objective: Leverage accumulated advantages to advance on all fronts
- Method: Strike decisively when conditions are ripe
- Mindset: Confident but not complacent
- Key action: Identify the turning point, seize the opportunity

### Step 4: Seek Offense Within Defense

> "Seek tactical offense within strategic defense" — "On Protracted War"《论持久战》

Even while in an overall difficult phase, take the initiative on specific, localized problems:
- Strategically it is a protracted war; tactically, seek quick decisions
- Concentrate forces on each specific small task for rapid completion
- Use one small victory after another to build confidence and advantage

### Step 5: Identify the Turning Point

> "The second stage... is the pivot of the whole situation" — "On Protracted War"《论持久战》

Continuously observe changing conditions and identify the moment to shift from stalemate to counteroffensive:
- Has accumulated knowledge become sufficient?
- Are resources and capabilities now in place?
- Have external conditions matured?
- Have the principal obstacles been removed?

When conditions are ripe, decisively shift to full-scale advancement.

### Step 6: Maintain Subjective Initiative

> "The decisive factor is man, not material" — "On Protracted War"《论持久战》

Throughout the entire process:
- Maintain an active attitude and proactive spirit
- Do not be overwhelmed by temporary difficulties
- Exploit every favorable condition
- Create advantage from disadvantage

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Mao's Critique | Correct Approach |
|------|------------|---------|
| Rushing for quick results | "Quick-victory theory" — blind optimism | Acknowledge that time is needed; advance in stages |
| Giving up when facing difficulty | "National-subjugation theory" — pessimistic surrender | Difficulties are temporary; persistence leads to victory |
| Passively waiting during the difficult phase | "Seek tactical offense within strategic defense" | Take the initiative even during difficult phases |
| Failing to recognize stage transitions | Must identify "the pivot of the whole situation" | Continuously assess and seize the turning point |
| Pushing recklessly without retreating | "A clever boxer usually gives a little ground" | Strategically retreat when necessary to preserve strength |

## Operating Procedure

When this skill is triggered, **output a stage assessment and action plan**:

1. **Determine the current stage** (must explicitly select one):
   - `Strategic Defense`: Objective is clear but capability/resources are temporarily insufficient; accumulation is needed first
   - `Strategic Stalemate`: A foundation has been established; accumulating advantages while awaiting the turning point
   - `Strategic Counteroffensive`: Conditions are ripe; full-scale advancement is possible

2. **Complete the stage assessment form**:
   ```
   Current Stage: [Strategic Defense / Stalemate / Counteroffensive]
   Basis for Judgment: ...

   Our Advantages: ...
   Our Disadvantages: ...
   Key Obstacles: ...

   Core Task for This Stage (1 item): ...
   Prohibited Actions for This Stage: ... (to prevent quick-victory-ism or defeatism)

   Conditions for Transitioning to the Next Stage: When [specific condition], transition to [next stage]
   ```

3. **During the Defense/Stalemate stage, a "localized offensive target" must also be specified**: i.e., while in an overall accumulation phase, identify which specific small problem can be immediately tackled with concentrated forces (invoke `concentrate-forces`).

4. **Set stage checkpoints**: Explicitly state "After completing [specific milestone], re-invoke this skill to reassess the stage."

- **Practice-Cognition**: Each stage is a cycle of practice and cognition
- **Overall Planning**: Resource allocation across the three stages requires overall planning

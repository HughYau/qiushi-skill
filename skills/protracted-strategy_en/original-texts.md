# Protracted Strategy — Original Textual Basis

### "On Protracted War"《论持久战》(May 1938)

**Opposing two erroneous arguments:**
> "The War of Resistance Against Japan is a protracted war, and the final victory will be China's — this is our conclusion."
> He opposed both the "theory of national subjugation" (pessimistic capitulation) and the "theory of quick victory" (blind optimism).

**The three stages:**
> Stage one — "the period of the enemy's strategic offensive and our strategic defensive"
> Stage two — "the period of the enemy's strategic consolidation and our preparation for the counter-offensive," i.e., the stage of strategic stalemate
> Stage three — "the period of our strategic counter-offensive and the enemy's strategic retreat"

**The strategic stalemate is the key:**
> "This second stage will be the transitional stage of the entire war and also the most difficult period, but it is the pivot of the whole war."

**Subjective initiative:**
> "The outcome of a war is mainly determined by the military, political, economic, and natural conditions on both sides — there is no question about that. But not by these alone; it is also determined by the subjective ability of the commanders on both sides."
> "Weapons are an important factor in war, but not the decisive factor; it is people, not things, that are decisive."

**Flexibility and mobility:**
> Seek tactical offensives within a strategic defensive; seek quick decisions within a protracted war; seek exterior-line operations within interior-line campaigns.

### "Problems of Strategy in China's Revolutionary War"《中国革命战争的战略问题》(December 1936)

**The wisdom of strategic retreat:**
> "Strategic retreat is a planned strategic step taken by an inferior force when facing the offensive of a superior force, in order to preserve its military strength and await an opportunity to defeat the enemy, because a swift counter-blow is not possible."
> "Everyone knows that when two boxers fight, a clever boxer usually gives a little ground at first, while the foolish one rushes in furiously and uses all his tricks at the start — only to end up being beaten by the one who gave ground."

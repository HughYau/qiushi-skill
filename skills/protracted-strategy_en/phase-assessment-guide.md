# Protracted Strategy Phase Assessment Guide

Use in conjunction with the `protracted-strategy` skill. Helps determine the current phase of a long-term task and select the corresponding strategy.

## Three Phases at a Glance

| Phase | Core Characteristics | Correct Strategy | Incorrect Strategy |
|-------|---------------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Strategic Defensive | Insufficient information, limited capability, in the exploration period | Preserve strength, investigate and research, accumulate understanding | Rushing into an all-out offensive |
| Strategic Stalemate | Some foundation but breakthrough conditions not yet met; the most difficult phase | Accumulate small victories into a large victory; advance patiently | Passive waiting or reckless advance |
| Strategic Offensive | Conditions are ripe, advantages have been sufficiently accumulated | Push forward decisively, launch a full-scale effort | Hesitating and missing the window of opportunity |

## Phase Identification Indicators

### Signals That You Are in the **Strategic Defensive** Phase

- [ ] Understanding of the problem domain is still superficial; new unknowns are frequently discovered
- [ ] Attempted solutions frequently fail or require major adjustments
- [ ] No reliable infrastructure/toolchain to depend on
- [ ] Low confidence in the task among the team/individual
- [ ] External constraints (unclear requirements, missing dependencies) limit the room for action

**What you should do in this phase:**
- Conduct extensive investigation and research (`investigation-first`)
- Establish a minimal viable base area (`spark-prairie-fire`)
- Allow strategic retreat — a skillful boxer first steps back
- Do not pursue perfection; aim to "gain a firm foothold"

### Signals That You Are in the **Strategic Stalemate** Phase

- [ ] Initial understanding and foundation are in place, but there is still a clear gap to the goal
- [ ] Progress exists but is slow; feelings of anxiety or fatigue are common
- [ ] Some "tough nut" problems remain unsolved
- [ ] External conditions are unlikely to change significantly in the near term
- [ ] Accumulated experience is beginning to prove useful, but is not yet sufficient for a decisive breakthrough

**What you should do in this phase:**
- Concentrate forces for quick resolution on each specific problem (`concentrate-forces`)
- Accumulate small victories into a large victory; maintain the pace of progress
- This is "the most difficult period, yet it is the turning point" — persevere
- Continuously watch for signals of a turning point

### Signals That You Are in the **Strategic Offensive** Phase

- [ ] Understanding of the problem is deep; the solution is clear and reliable
- [ ] Infrastructure and tools are in place; execution efficiency is high
- [ ] Major obstacles have been eliminated or significantly weakened
- [ ] Team/individual confidence is strong; execution capability is robust
- [ ] External conditions are favorable; a window of opportunity has appeared

**What you should do in this phase:**
- Push forward decisively and comprehensively; do not hesitate
- Maintain momentum; sustain continuous operations
- Still pay tactical attention to every detail
- Do not relax just because victory is near

## Key Signals of Phase Transition

### Defensive → Stalemate
- A systematic understanding of the problem has been achieved (unexpected surprises are no longer frequent)
- Reliable infrastructure/base areas have been established
- Local problems can be resolved consistently

### Stalemate → Offensive
- Accumulated small victories trigger a quantitative-to-qualitative transformation
- A critical obstacle has been broken through
- External conditions shift favorably
- Execution confidence and capability reach a critical threshold

### Note: Transition Is Not Instantaneous
> "The second stage is the transitional stage of the entire war."

Phase transition is gradual. Do not expect a clear signal that says "now is the time to go on the offensive." Assess continuously; when multiple indicators are satisfied simultaneously, the moment has arrived.

## Common Misjudgments

| Misjudgment | Actual Situation | Correction |
|-------------|-----------------|------------|
| "Ready to go on the offensive" (actually still in stalemate) | Overconfidence from one or two small victories | Check whether multiple offensive-phase indicators are met |
| "Still in the defensive phase" (actually already in stalemate) | Excessive caution; reluctance to push forward | Assess whether a stable base area has already been established |
| "No progress at all" (actually progressing normally in the stalemate phase) | Impatience with the slow progress inherent to the stalemate phase | The stalemate phase is inherently the most difficult and also the most critical |

# Concentrating Forces — Original Textual Basis

### "Problems of Strategy in China's Revolutionary War"《中国革命战争的战略问题》(December 1936)

**The principle of concentrating forces:**
> "Our strategy is 'pit one against ten,' and our tactics are 'pit ten against one' — this is one of our fundamental laws for defeating the enemy."
> "When the enemy advances in several columns, we generally adopt the policy of defeating them one by one."

**Annihilation rather than mere rout:**
> "Our policy from the very first stage of the war has been to oppose the strategy of pursuit and to advocate the strategy of annihilation." "Routing ten enemy divisions is not as effective as annihilating one of them."

**Fight no battle unprepared:**
> "In every battle, concentrate an absolutely superior force (two, three, four, and sometimes even five or six times the enemy's strength), encircle the enemy on all sides, and strive to annihilate them completely, allowing none to escape."

### "Ten Major Principles of Operation"《十大军事原则》(December 1947)

> "(1) Attack dispersed and isolated enemies first; attack concentrated and strong enemies later."
> "(2) Take small and medium cities and extensive rural areas first; take big cities later."
> "(3) Make the annihilation of the enemy's effective strength the main objective; do not make the holding or seizing of cities and territory the main objective."
> "(4) In every battle, concentrate an absolutely superior force... encircle the enemy on all sides, and strive to annihilate them completely, allowing none to escape. Under unfavorable conditions... strive to annihilate a portion of them."
> "(6) Foster the style of fighting with courage, with no fear of sacrifice, no fear of fatigue, and continuous operations (that is, fighting several battles in succession in a short time without rest)."
> "(9) Make good use of the intervals between campaigns to rest and consolidate troops. The period of rest and consolidation should generally not be too long."

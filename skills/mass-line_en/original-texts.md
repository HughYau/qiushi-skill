# Mass Line — Original Textual Basis

### "Some Questions Concerning Methods of Leadership"《关于领导方法的若干问题》(June 1943)

**The complete formulation of the mass line:**
> "Take the ideas of the masses, concentrate them, then go back to the masses to persevere in carrying them out, so as to form correct leadership — this is the basic method of leadership. In the process of concentrating and persevering, it is necessary to combine general calls with particular guidance."

**Combining the general with the particular:**
> "No matter what work task is involved, without a general and universal call, the broad masses cannot be mobilized for action. But if those in leadership roles confine themselves to a general call without concretely and directly implementing the work called for in a number of organizations, breaking through at one point, gaining experience, and then using that experience to guide other units, they will have no way of testing whether their general call is correct, nor will they be able to enrich the content of that general call, and there is the danger that it will come to nothing."

### "Preface and Postscript to Rural Surveys"《〈农村调查〉的序言和跋》(March and April 1941)

> "The masses are the real heroes, while we ourselves are often childish and ignorant. Without understanding this, it is impossible to acquire even the most basic knowledge."

### "On Coalition Government"《论联合政府》(April 1945)

> "We should maintain the closest ties with the broadest masses of the people. We should serve the people wholeheartedly, never for a moment divorcing ourselves from the masses; everything should proceed from the interests of the people rather than from the interests of individuals or small groups; we should be accountable to the people and to the Party's leading organs alike — these are our starting points."

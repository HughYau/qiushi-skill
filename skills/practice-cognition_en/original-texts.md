# Theory of Practice and Cognition — Original Textual Basis

### "On Practice"《实践论》(July 1937)

**Knowledge comes from practice:**
> "If you want to know the taste of a pear, you must change it by eating it yourself." "If you want to know the structure and properties of an atom, you must conduct physical and chemical experiments to alter the state of the atom." "If you want to know the theory and methods of revolution, you must participate in revolution. All genuine knowledge originates in direct experience."

**The two leaps of cognition:**

The first leap — from perceptual knowledge to rational knowledge:
> "As social practice continues, things that gave rise to people's perceptions and impressions in practice are repeated many times; then a sudden change (a leap) occurs in the process of cognition in the human brain, and concepts are formed." "Concepts are no longer the phenomena of things, no longer their individual aspects, no longer their external connections, but have grasped the essence of things, their totality, and their internal connections."

The second leap — from rational knowledge to practice (the more important one):
> "The active role of cognition manifests itself not only in the active leap from perceptual to rational knowledge, but — and this is more important — it must manifest itself in the leap from rational knowledge to revolutionary practice."

**Practice is the sole criterion for testing truth:**
> "Whether a cognition or theory is truth is determined not by subjective feelings but by the objective results of social practice. The criterion of truth can only be social practice."

### "Where Do Correct Ideas Come From?"《人的正确思想是从哪里来的》(May 1963)

> "Where do correct ideas come from? Do they drop from the skies? No. Are they innate in the mind? No. Correct ideas can only come from social practice — only from three kinds of practice: the struggle for production, the class struggle, and scientific experiment."
> "A correct understanding often requires many repetitions of the process leading from matter to consciousness and from consciousness to matter, that is, from practice to knowledge and from knowledge to practice, before it can be completed."

### "Reform Our Study"《改造我们的学习》(May 1941)

**Opposing dogmatism:**
> "There is a couplet that serves as a portrait of such people. It reads: 'Reeds on the wall — top-heavy, thin-stemmed, and shallow-rooted; bamboo shoots in the hills — sharp-tongued, thick-skinned, and hollow inside.'" "Those with book knowledge should develop in the direction of practice."

**Opposing empiricism:**
> "Those with work experience should study theory and read conscientiously; only then can their experience become systematic and comprehensive, rising to the level of theory, and only then will they not mistake their partial experience for universal truth."

**Seeking truth from facts:**
> "'Facts' are all the things that objectively exist; 'truth' means their internal connections, that is, their laws; and 'to seek' means that we study them. We should proceed from the actual conditions at home and abroad, inside and outside the province, inside and outside the county, inside and outside the district, derive from them their inherent rather than imaginary laws — that is, find the internal connections of the events around us — and use these as a guide for our action."
